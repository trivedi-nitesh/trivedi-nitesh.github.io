About Me

I am a Ph.D. student in the Department of Computer Science and Engineering at IIT Kanpur, where I work with Prof. Subhajit Roy. I come from Lucknow, a city with a famously amusing food philosophy: chicken is considered vegetarian if you wear it and non-vegetarian only if you eat it.

My research lies at the intersection of Machine Learning and Formal Methods, focusing on improving the reliability of complex software systems. I am particularly interested in integrating learning-based techniques with rigorous program verification to achieve scalable automation. This direction has led me to develop tools and approaches for analyzing probabilistic programs, reasoning about data-centric systems, and exploring fairness in machine learning models.

Research Interests

  • Machine Learning and its applications in software systems
  • Formal Methods and program verification
  • Data-driven analysis and probabilistic programs
  • Fairness and interpretability in AI
